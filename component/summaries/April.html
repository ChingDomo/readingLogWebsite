April 2004

After

a long term struggleing, Alice and John made the difficult decision to inform their children about Alice's diagnosis with Alzheimer's disease. Anna expressed deep concern for her own children, fearing that they too might be at risk of inheriting the disease. Tom emphasized the importance of understanding the diagnosis and its implications. Lydia, on the other hand, cared about how Alice feeled.

Anna and Tom both expressed a desire to undergo genetic testing to determine if they carried the mutation associated with Alzheimer's disease. They hoped that by knowing their genetic status, they could gain a better understanding of their own potential risk. However, Lydia chose not to undergo testing, possibly due to her own personality or thinking about Alice's feeling.

The test results revealed that Tom did not possess the DNA associated with Alzheimaer's, bringing some relief to the family. However, the outcome for Anna was different, as she tested positive for the test, indicating a 100% risk of developing Alzheimer's in the future.

The news of Anna's positive test result added another layer of complexity and concern within the family. They now had to grapple with the knowledge that Alice's disease might have a genetic component, potentially affecting future generations. The weight of this realization further intensified the emotional and uncertain journey that the family would face together.
