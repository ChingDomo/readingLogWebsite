January 2004

On

January nineteenth, a day that held deep significance for Alice as it marked the anniversary of her mother and sister's tragic deaths. On the same day, she had an appointment with Dr. Davis. Ironically, it was on this unlucky day that Alice received the devastating diagnosis of early-onset Alzheimer's disease. And Dr. Davis urged her to share the news with John, her husband.

Alice and John visited the cemetery where her mother and sister were laid to rest. As she stood there, memories flooded back of the drunk driving accident that claimed her mother and sister's lives, which was caused by her father. Now, in a cruel twist of fate, Alice found herself battling this terrible disease, seemingly passed down through her father's DNA. The anger and resentment she harbored towards her father intensified, as she saw him as the perpetrator of their family's tragedy.

Ten days after her diagnosis, Alice mustered the courage to share the truth with John. However, he struggled to come to terms with the reality of the situation. In an attempt to deny the heartbreaking truth, he asked Alice to double-check with Dan's wife, hoping for a glimmer of hope.

In this moment, the weight of the diagnosis and the challenges ahead weighed heavily on Alice and John, as they grappled with the harsh truth and tried to find a way to navigate this new chapter in their lives.
