September 2003

First chapter of Still Alice.

Alice,

a highly analytical and exceptional professor, possessed an unwavering focus on time management. In stark contrast, her husband John was constantly rushing to locate his belongings, often in a state of disarray.

Despite Alice's remarkable career achievements, her former Harvard classmate and rival, Josh, held an inflated sense of superiority over her. This became evident when he lashed out at Alice sarcastically during a conference, belittling her abilities. Caught off guard, Alice struggled to find her usual eloquence, as her thoughts slipped away on the stage.

The discord between Alice and her daughter Lydia regarding higher education created a continual source of disagreement between them. The clash over Lydia's career choices added an uncomfortable undertone to their interactions. During their meeting, Alice's discomfort with Lydia's living arrangements became apparent as she persistently questioned her about her roommate. And Alice sensed an underlying unease regarding Lydia's decision to live with two male roommates while working part-time at Starbucks.Upon returning to Cambridge, Alice realized that she had forgotten to pack her running shoes for her trip to California. As she attempted her usual jogging routine, she found herself disoriented in the once-familiar surroundings of Harvard Square, struggling to navigate a place she once knew like the back of her hand.

In an attempt to make sense of her lapses in memory, Alice persuaded herself that these symptoms were merely a result of menopause, downplaying their significance.

Little did Alice know, these seemingly isolated incidents marked the beginning of a journey that would challenge her understanding of herself, her relationships, and the true nature of her memory.
