July 2004

Summary and one discussin.

Alice

and John decided to spend the summer in Chatham. However, during one of Alice's walks along the beach, she felt drawn to the warm seawater and found herself walking into the sea. At that moment, thoughts of suicide briefly crossed her mind as she contemplated drifting away until she could no longer swim.

Hopefully, John returned just in time. Instead of scolding her, he joined her in the water, providing comfort and support.

Unfortunately, Alice's condition continued to deteriorate. She became confused, unable to remember her intended actions. As she tried to recall the need to use the restroom, she couldn't find it, and the distressing moment of incontinence was witnessed by John, which left Alice feeling utterly devastated.

While John attended the International Conference on Alzheimer's Disease, he tried to gather information to help Alice in his own way. However, Alice remained skeptical and doubted that he would find anything meaningful.

Alice had forgotten about her sister Anne's passing. When Lydia and John repeatedly telled her, Alice couldn't comprehend the reality and believed that Anne had only recently died, feeling left out of the loop. This lack of communication left Alice distrusting John.

What do you think Alice walked into the water?

I think that when Alice first walked into the sea, she would experience a sense of happiness and acceptance. She would discover that the water is pleasantly warm, and she might even wish to stay in the sea indefinitely. As Alice's condition has progressed, she has lost many abilities in her daily life. She was aware of this herself, and she realized that it is becoming increasingly difficult for her to interact with people she used to be close to. For example, John no longer asked her for help(find his glasses). It felt as if her illness had caused her to lose the Alice she once was. This realization added a touch of melancholy to her life, and she struggled to face how to continue living. Therefore, I believe Alice would want to stay in the sea because only the ocean can provide her with direct warmth and acceptance.

I have had my own experiences of walking into the water at the beach, and I believe the ocean possesses a certain magic. It is warm enough and the waves continuously caress every part of the body, allowing a person to relax or temporarily forget about the troubles of reality. It makes one want to keep walking towards the depths of the sea.
